üåçIntroduction

This report details a process simulation for the production of synthesis gas (syngas) and the subsequent separation of high-purity hydrogen. Syngas, a mixture primarily composed of hydrogen (H‚ÇÇ), carbon monoxide (CO), and some carbon dioxide (CO‚ÇÇ), is a crucial intermediate in the chemical industry. It serves as a feedstock for producing a wide range of valuable products, including ammonia, methanol, and synthetic fuels. The hydrogen separated from syngas is a vital component in various industrial processes, such as hydrocracking, and is a clean energy carrier with immense potential for a sustainable future. This simulation, performed in DWSIM, models the key unit operations involved in this process, providing valuable insights into stream properties and equipment performance.
