Process Flow Overview

Four-stage purification process converting biomass-derived syngas to ultra-pure hydrogen

1

Three-Stage Syngas Cooling

Cools syngas from 514K to 313K while recovering 5.34 MW of waste heat for steam generation and boiler feedwater preheating.

2

Acid Gas Removal (AGR)

Removes 89.6% of CO₂ via activated MDEA absorption with innovative "Bulk Removal" strategy optimized for downstream PSA processing.

3

Pressure Swing Adsorption (PSA)

Purifies hydrogen to 99.999% purity with 88% recovery using a four-bed, three-layer polybed system.

4

Polishing & Conditioning

Ensures final product safety through methanation, deep drying, and compression to 350 bar storage pressure.

Design Innovations

  • Integrated Bulk Removal Strategy: AGR optimized for 2.0% CO₂ slip reduces column height by 70% and reboiler duty by 60% compared to conventional designs.
  • Energy Integration: 7.88 MW heat recovery in AGR interchanger and 1.48 MW in polishing train thermal management.
  • Layered PSA Architecture: Three adsorbent layers handle dehydration, bulk CO₂/CH₄ removal, and CO polishing in a single vessel.
  • Safety Chain Approach: Methanation reactor provides final CO elimination barrier, ensuring product safety for fuel cell applications.

Design Conclusions

This integrated design demonstrates a technically robust and economically viable pathway for converting biomass-derived syngas to high-purity hydrogen, suitable for emerging clean energy applications. The complete plant delivers:

  • Capacity: 1.548 kg/s (5,573 kg/h) ultra-pure hydrogen
  • Purity: 99.9999% with CO < 0.1 ppmv
  • Conditions: 350 bar, -70°C dew point
  • Economic Value: $122 million/year at current market prices

The design represents a state-of-the-art hydrogen purification facility integrating advanced separation technologies with rigorous safety systems and energy optimization, making it suitable for commercial implementation in Bolu Province based on local biomass resources.
