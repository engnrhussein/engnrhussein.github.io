Executive Summary

The objective of this design project was to develop a complete automated control system for a Jacketed Continuous Stirred Tank Reactor (CSTR) producing Ethyl Acetate. The design ensures a production rate of 300 L/min at 56% conversion while maintaining strict safety and quality standards.

The reaction kinetics were modeled as a second-order reversible esterification. A linear stability analysis determined the process time constants (τₜ = 13.3 min) and process gains, which were used to tune the controllers. The final control strategy utilizes a Feedback PID loop for temperature control, Ratio Control for feed stoichiometry, and fail-safe interlocks for pressure and level.

The proposed design meets all operational requirements (80°C, 70% Level, 1.5 bar) and includes comprehensive safety layers to prevent thermal runaway or vessel overpressure.

Control Loop Interactions

Primary Coupling Effects:

  • Temperature-Concentration Coupling: Temperature affects reaction rate via Arrhenius equation (k ∝ e⁻ᴱᵃ/ᴿᵀ)
  • Level-Residence Time Coupling: τ = V/Q = Ah/Q → Level changes affect residence time
  • Pressure-Temperature Coupling: Temperature affects vapor pressure (Antoine equation)
  • Feed Flow Interactions: Acid and ethanol flows must maintain 1:1 stoichiometric ratio
  • Steam-Jacket Interactions: Steam flow directly controls reactor temperature

Design Validation

The proposed control system design successfully addresses all process requirements:

  • Production Quality: Temperature control at 80°C ±2°C optimizes reaction kinetics without triggering side reactions
  • Stoichiometric Efficiency: Ratio control maintains 1:1 molar feed ratio within ±2%
  • Process Stability: Level control maintains 70% liquid level for constant residence time (τ = 13.3 min)
  • Equipment Safety: Pressure control limits reactor to 1.5 bar with mechanical relief at 5.0 bar

The design incorporates comprehensive safety layers including fail-safe valve configurations, alarm interlocks, and mechanical relief protection to prevent thermal runaway or vessel overpressure. All control loops are tuned based on linearized process models derived from first principles, ensuring robust performance under normal operating conditions.
